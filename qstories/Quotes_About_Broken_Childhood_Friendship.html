Quotes About Broken Childhood Friendship

1. “Breaking with old friends is one of the most painful of the changes in all that piling up of a multitude of small distasteful changes that constitutes growing older.” – John Dos Passos

2. “People change. Friends may become strangers with time, but the memories will always stay the same.” – Unknown

3. “Old friends become bitter enemies all of a sudden for toys and small offenses.” – Robert Burton

4. “He felt lost and alone without his old friend, though he barely recognized him anymore. He wondered if they could ever be friends again.” – Erin Hunter

5. “Men kick friendship around like a football, but it doesn’t seem to crack. Women treat it like glass and it goes to pieces.” – Anne Morrow Lindbergh

6. “It is one of the major tragedies that nothing is more discomforting than the hearty affection of the Old Friends who never were friends.” – Sinclair Lewis

7. “As soon as you get a certain amount of attention, then everybody kinda wants to start taking potshots at you. All your old friends that supported you don’t support you anymore.” – John Zorn

8. “It’s awkward going back up to Edinburgh to see my old friends, because I’m not on the same wavelength.” – James William Middleton

9. “Growing apart does not change the fact that for a long time we grew side by side; our roots will always be tangled. I am glad for that.” – Ally Condie

10. “It’s no good trying to keep up old friendships. It’s painful for both sides. The fact is, one grows out of people, and the only thing is to face it.” – W. Somerset Maugham

11. “But now old friends are acting strange. They shake their heads, they say I’ve changed. Well, something’s lost, but something’s gained in living every day.” – Joni Mitchell

12. “It’s less the words they say than those they leave unsaid that split old friends apart.” – Frederick Buechner

13. “Many of my old friends are gone now. I have a hard time dealing with the fact that they’re just not there to talk to. I can’t call them up for a rabbit-skin glue recipe anymore.” – James Rosenquist

14. “Sweet is the memory of distant friends! Like the mellow rays of the departing sun, it falls tenderly, yet sadly, on the heart.” – Washington Irving

15. “Old books that have ceased to be of service should no more be abandoned than should old friends who have ceased to give pleasure.” – Bernard Baru

16. “True friendship is like sound health; the value of it is seldom known until it is lost.” – Charles Caleb Colton

17. “Sometimes being a friend means mastering the art of timing. There is a time for silence. A time to let go and allow people to hurl themselves into their own destiny. And a time to prepare to pick up the pieces when it’s all over.” – Octavia Butler

18. “Friends aren’t forever. They’re not even for a while. They come into your life and they leave when something or someone changes.” – Krista Ritchie
