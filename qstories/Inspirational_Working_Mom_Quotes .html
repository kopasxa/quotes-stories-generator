Inspirational Working Mom Quotes 

1. “She never quite leaves her children at home, even when she doesn’t take them along.” – Margaret Culkin Banning

2. “A happy mother is a good mother, and if work makes you hum, your whole family sings along.” – Sharon Meers and Joanna Strober

3. “For me, being a mother made me a better professional because coming home every night to my girls reminded me of what I was working for. And being a professional made me a better mother because by pursuing my dreams, I was modeling for my girls how to pursue their dreams.” – Michelle Obama

4. “I think every working mom probably feels the same thing. You go through big chunks of time where you’re just thinking, ‘This is impossible, oh, this is impossible.’ And then you just keep going and keep going, and you sort of do the impossible.” – Tina Fey

5. “I think moms put a lot of pressure on ourselves trying to balance it all. It’s never going to be perfectly balanced. The sooner you know this, the sooner you can relieve some of the pressure you put on yourself.” – Denise Richards

6. “Trying to do it all and expecting that it can be done exactly right is a recipe for disappointment. Perfection is the enemy.” – Sheryl Sandberg

7. “Start by doing what’s necessary; then do what’s possible, and suddenly you are doing the impossible.” – St. Francis of Assisi

8. “The balancing act of motherhood and a career, and being a wife, is something that I don’t think I’ll ever perfect, but I love the challenge of it.” – Kerri Walsh Jennings

9. “The attitude you have as a parent is what your kids will learn from more than what you tell them. They don’t remember what you try to teach them. They remember what you are.” – Jim Henson

10. “Knowing I’ve got this beautiful baby to go home to makes me feel like I don’t have to play another match. I don’t need the money or the titles or the prestige. I want them, but I don’t need them. That’s a different feeling for me.” – Serena Williams

11. “Making changes in our careers and lives is just that – change. It doesn’t mean throwing anything away.” – Becca Carnahan

12. “It’s not difficult to take care of a child; it’s difficult to do anything else while taking care of a child.” – Julianne Moore

13. “It’s not what you do for your children but what you have taught them to do for themselves that will make them successful human beings.” – Ann Landers

14. “I think while all mothers deal with guilt, working mothers are plagued by guilt on steroids.” – Arianna Huffington 

15. “We should go after our dreams and not be apologetic about it, but it’s scary. Whether you want to work or not, you have to do what makes you a fuller person. You have to love yourself.” – Catherine Reitman

16. “I’m a woman, of course, I still have curves on me, and that’s ok. I had a baby and I’ve worked hard. And I don’t think women should have to feel that kind of pressure. I’ve done it really healthfully, and I took my time.” – Lily Aldridge

17. “Buckle up, and know that it’s going to be a tremendous amount of work, but embrace it.” – Tory Burch

18. “Life is not easy for any of us. But what of that? We must have perseverance and above all confidence in ourselves. We must believe that we are gifted for something and that this thing must be attained.” – Marie Curie

19. “When you are a mother, you are never really alone in your thoughts. A mother always has to think twice, once for herself and once for her child.” – Sophia Loren

20. “Your most valuable parenting skill is learning to manage yourself first.” – Dr. Laura Markham

21. “When I started law school, my daughter, Jane, was 14 months and I attribute my success in law school largely to Jane. I went to class at about 8:30 am and I came home at 4:00 pm, that was the children’s hour. It was a total break in my day and children’s hour continued until Jane went to sleep. Then I was happy to go back to the books, so I felt each part of my life gave me respite from the other.” – Ruth Bader Ginsburg
