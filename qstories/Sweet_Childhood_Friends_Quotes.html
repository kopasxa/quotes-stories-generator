Sweet Childhood Friends Quotes

1. “It’s the friends you can call up at 4 a.m. that matter.” – Marlene Dietrich

2. “A good friend is someone who sees the pain in your eyes while everyone else believes the smile on your face.” – Unknown

3. “To the world, you may be just one person, but to one person you may be the world.” – Unknown

4. “There’s no friend like someone who has known you since you were five.” – Anne Stevenson

5. “Tell me, why are the old friends kind and ever the tenderest, too? Youth has no art, but an open mind, And its love is sincere and true.” – Edwin Leibfreed

6. “When you get lost in a really strange place, nothing is more comforting than found your friend whom you trust and can show the way.” – Toba Beta

7. “I’d like to be the sort of friend that you have been to me. I’d like to be the help that you’ve been always glad to be; I’d like to mean as much to you each minute of the day, as you have meant, an old friend of mine, to me along the way.” – Edgar Guest

8. “To be with old friends is very warming and comforting.” – Ian Ziering

9. “The secret to a really great friendship is just creating fun memories whenever you’re with that person.” – Zendaya Coleman

10. “We didn’t realize we were making memories, we just knew we were having fun.” – Unknown

11. “Friendship marks a life even more deeply than love. Love risks degenerating into obsession, friendship is never anything but sharing.” – Elie Wiesel

12. “Beautiful memories are like old friends. They may not always be on your mind, but they are forever in your heart.” – Susan Gale

13. “I think if I’ve learned anything about friendship, it’s to hang in, stay connected, fight for them, and let them fight for you. Don’t walk away, don’t be distracted, don’t be too busy or tired, don’t take them for granted. Friends are part of the glue that holds life and faith together. Powerful stuff.” – John Katz

14. “Friendship can start in childhood and follow you through your whole life.” – Unknown

15. “There’s no distance or time that can set us apart, my friend, as long as you have my trust.” – Unknown

16. “Ten minutes with a genuine friend is better than years spent with anyone less.” – Crystal Woods

17. “Friendship is always a sweet responsibility, never an opportunity.” – Khalil Gibran

18. “There is nothing better than the encouragement of a good old friend.” – Katharine Butler Hathaway

19. “A simple friend thinks the friendship over when you argue. A real friend knows that it’s not a friendship until after you’ve fought.” – Unknown

20. “I love coming back home and seeing old friends and family. I would say it keeps me grounded.” – Coy Bowles

21. “There are friendships imprinted in our hearts that will never be diminished by time and distance.” – Dodinsky

22. “Don’t expect your friend to be a perfect person but help your friend to become a perfect person. That’s true friendship.”– Mother Teresa

23. “Old friends die on you, and they’re irreplaceable. You become dependent.” – Lionel Blue

24. “There is nothing I wouldn’t do for those who are my friends. I have no notion of loving people by halves; it is not my nature.” – Jane Austen

25. “Friendship is a single soul dwelling in two bodies.” – Aristotle

26. “When I see you through my eyes, I think that we are different. When I see you through my heart, I know we are the same.” – Doe Zantamata

27. “A friend is someone who knows all about you and still loves you.” – Elbert Hubbard

28. “A true friend is like an umbrella that opens her heart to protect you on those rainy days.” – Debasish Mridha

29. “Ah, how good it feels! The hand of an old friend.” – Henry Wadsworth Longfellow

30. “Friendship is the source of the greatest pleasures, and without friends even the most agreeable pursuits become tedious.” – Thomas Aquinas

31. “Wishing to be friends is quick work, but friendship is a slow ripening fruit.” – Aristotle

32. “When I see old friends, I’m very excited.” – Chino Moreno

33. “An old friend never can be found, and nature has provided that he cannot easily be lost.” – Samuel Johnson

34. “A true friend is someone who thinks that you are a good egg even though he knows that you are slightly cracked.” – Bernard Meltzer

35. “Some people arrive and make such a beautiful impact on your life; you can barely remember what life was like without them.” – Anna Taylor

36. “I love everything that’s old, – old friends, old times, old manners, old books, and old wine.” – Oliver Goldsmith

37. “That friend who you may not see very often, but the moment you reconnect feels like yesterday.” – Unknown

38. “Friends are like walls, sometimes you lean on them, and sometimes it’s good just knowing they’re there.” – Unknown

39. “Don’t be dismayed by good-byes. A farewell is necessary before you can meet again. And meeting again, after moments or lifetimes, is certain for those who are friends.” – Richard Bach

40. “Are we not like two volumes of one book?” – Marceline Desbordes-Valmore

41. “Friendship improves happiness and abates misery, by the doubling of our joy and the dividing of our grief.” – Marcus Tullius Cicero

42. “Friends are the angels who lift us to our feet when our wings have trouble remembering how to fly.” – Lorraine K. Mitchell

43. “Friendship is a gift that is fair in all things. It roots from one’s heart and involves memories that stay not for a while but a lifetime.” – Unknown

44. “Each friend represents a world in us, a world possibly not born until they arrive, and it is only by this meeting that a new world is born.” – Anais Nin

45. “Remember that the most valuable antiques are dear old friends.” – H. Jackson Brown

46. “There is something about childhood friends that you just can’t replace.” – Lisa Whelchel

47. “If ever there is a tomorrow when we’re not together, there is something you must always remember: You are braver than you believe, stronger than you seem, and smarter than you think.” – A.A. Milne

48. “If you carry your childhood with you, you never become older.” – Tom Stoppard

49. “We do not remember days, we remember moments.” – Cesare Pavese

50. “The glory of friendship is not the outstretched hand, nor the kindly smile, nor the joy of companionship; it is the spiritual inspiration that comes to one when he discovers that someone else believes in him and is willing to trust him with friendship.” – Ralph Waldo Emerson

51. “Friends are the most important part of your life. Treasure the tears, treasure the laughter, but most importantly, treasure the memories.” – Dave Brenner

52. “There is one friend in the life of each of us, who seems not a separate person, however dear and beloved, but an expansion, an interpretation, of one’s self, the very meaning of one’s soul.” – Edith Wharton

53. “I love the friends that you don’t see for days, weeks even months and the bond is still strong as ever.” – Unknown

54. “If you live to be a hundred, I want to live to be a hundred minus one day so I never have to live without you.” – A.A. Milne

55. “Friendship is a special kind of love. More than endless, more than true. When friends parted ways no matter the distance, expect them to keep coming back to you.” – John K.

56. “The greatest sweetener of human life is friendship. To raise this to the highest pitch of enjoyment is a secret which but few discover.” – Joseph Addison

57. “There is only one thing better than making a new friend and that is keeping an old one.” – Elmer G. Letterman

58. “Childhood friendships are a key building block for relationships in later life. They hone skills for future relationships, demonstrate the importance of emotional commitment, ease the separation from the family, safeguard against feelings of rejection and loneliness and ease the transition to adulthood.” – Molly Countermine

59. “I speak to my childhood friends almost every day over the Internet.” – Alessandra Ambrosi

60. “I have always looked at life as a voyage, mostly wonderful, sometimes frightening. In my family and friends, I have discovered treasure more valuable than gold.” – Jimmy Buffett

61. “If friendship is your weakest point then you are the strongest person in the world.” – Abraham Lincoln

62. “So long as the memory of certain beloved friends lives in my heart, I shall say that life is good.” – Helen Keller

63. “The life of a model isn’t easy. But I try to keep a good head on my shoulders by staying close to my family and old friends. They’re my support system.” – Constance Jablonski
